🧪 Foundation Era (1920s-1980s)
Early statistical thinking about causation. Fisher's randomized experiments and Neyman's potential outcomes framework laid the groundwork for modern causal inference.
🎓 Three Schools Emerge (1970s-2000s)
Rubin's potential outcomes, Pearl's graphical models, and econometric natural experiments develop as distinct but related approaches to causal inference.
🌟 Modern Synthesis (2000s-2010s)
The three schools begin to converge. Matching methods, DAG-based identification, and econometric techniques are unified under common frameworks.
🤖 Machine Learning Era (2010s-present)
High-dimensional data and ML methods transform causal inference. Double ML, meta-learners, and neural approaches handle complex, heterogeneous effects.
💻 Software & Applications (2010s-present)
Open-source tools democratize causal methods. Industry applications at tech companies, healthcare, and policy evaluation scale to massive datasets.
